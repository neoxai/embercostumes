More Resources

Here are some websites that will help you along your way to costuming Glory!



General Costumes/References

The Costumer's Manifesto Lots of Great Tutorials and Tips for making costumes on a budget.

The Costumer's Guide A plentiful resrource for reference images from movies

The Padawan's Guide Everything you need to know to make a Star Wars costume including references and tutorials.

Cosplay.com A haven for Cosplayers to post pictures of completed anime-based costumes.

Cosplaylab.com A place for Cosplayers to convene and show off, includes forums, pictures of costumes, and includes anime, fantasy, and video game costumes.

Narniaweb: The Wardrobe Door References and tutorials for costumes from the Chronicles of Narnia Movies. Includes a message board for idea swapping.

Craftster.org A jack-of-all-crafts website with helpful sewing and costume tutorials, also a good place to get props advice.

AlleyCatScratch: Lord of the Rings Good Lord of the Rings resource, and many techniques can be applied to other costumes.



Historical Costumes

Sense and Sensibility Patterns She sells patterns for Regency-Titanic eras, and offers some great advice on period clothing.

The Rennaisance Tailor A great place chock full of Rennaisance tutorial goodness.



Hair Help

Imaginary Styles Need hair help? This site has instructions for some of the wackiest fantasy and sci-fi hairstyles.



Leather Working

Leatherworker.net The forums here are great for anyone interested in leatherworking. Lots of pictures, explanations and tutorials.

Hardware-Elf The one site that greedy leather salesmen don't want you to know about: hard to find and really cool latches and buckles, for leather or other materials.

Tandy Leather See if you can find a brick-and-mortar store in your town, because the employees are helpful and leather is better to pick out in person. But if you know what you want, their online catalog is extensive.

Armor Geek From the guy who runs InventGeek, except medieval leather armor projects.



Armor Making

The Armor Archive Great Medieval armor help. Most patterns work with foam, fiberglass, or metal.

Butted Mail: A Mailmaker's Guide The first place I point anyone who wants to start making chainmail.

M.A.I.L. Once you have the basics of chainmail, this site is full of variations and essays to help you along.

The Ring Lord One-stop-shopping for chainmail supplies of all kinds.



Props Help

Fiberglass Tutorial Useful, covers all steps well.

Instructables Cool hardware and electronics tutorials.
