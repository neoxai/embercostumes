Safety

Safety Glasses

I wear safety glasses anytime I'm in the workshop, no matter what the task. Regular eyeglasses aren't enough, get full-coverage wrapping clear safety glasses.

Dust Mask

Metal dust can cause respiratory problems; always wear a dust mask when filing, sanding, grinding or polishing metal.

Respirator

While a simple dust mask is fine for airborne particles, it does nothing against fumes. Spend the money on a respirator and always use it around bondo, fiberglass, spray paint and anything that gives of chemical fumes. The cartidges don't last forever, so make sure to change them frequently.

Earplugs

Protect your hearing, it cannot be restored once lost. Reusable earbud earplugs are comfortable and inexpensive, and should be worn for any loud operations.

Gloves

Wear insulating gloves when handling hot or sharp metal. Make sure they fit tight so they don't catch on any moving parts. Rubber gloves should be used with adhesives and casting materials.

Face Shield

Power tools can be a great asset, but care needs to be exercised around them. I recommend a full face shield when working with grinders or spark throwing equipment. Closed toed shoes are a must.

Loose Clothing

Tuck in shirts and roll up sleeves if you are near spinning or cutting power tools. Always be aware of moving parts and your own extremities.
