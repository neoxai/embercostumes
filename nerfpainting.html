Painting NERF guns

NERF guns make great props for cosplays, and serve practical purposes in LARP games such as Aftermath and Humans vs. Zombies.

The best spray paint available in the US for plastics is Rustoleum

1.) Disassemble blaster, keeping track of screws, springs, and small parts. I will make a chart or diagram of what size screws and small pieces go back where so that I can reassemble it correctly. Then, stash all the internal parts in a plastic bag or tackle box tray so that you can keep them all in one spot.

2.) Remove logos, warnings, unwanted text or texture. I'm not a fan of the faux-digital camo pattern on the new Elite series blasters, and the NERF and N-Strike logos are not a part of the props I'm trying to create. I go over the high parts with a dremel using a coarse sanding drum, and the lower designs with 150-220 grit sandpaper.

3.) Clean up scratch marks around the freshly sanded areas with 200-400 grit sandpaper.

4.) Wash all dust off and let dry completely.

5.) Using Automotive primer, spray a few coats until the entire thing is solid grey. The first coat should be a very light dusting, successive coats can be slightly thicker, as long as the primer doesn't pool or run. 6.) Spray the entire gun with the lightest color you plan on using. Use repeated light coats to avoid pooling of the paint.

7.) Tape off sections that are keeping their color, and add other the darker shades on top. I like 3M blue tape with Edge-lock.
(For cool effects like camoflage, see additional page (TO BE ADDED).) 8.) When all colors are finished, spray with a coat or two of clear coat. Semi-gloss or satin furniture coating works great.

8.) Reassemble the blaster. Test that it still works.

NOTE: Normal wear and tear will rub parts of the paint off, and it will need to be touched up from time to time.

OPTIONAL: If the blaster is going to be used at conventions, it is recommended that firing mechanisms are removed. Some conventions have strict weapons policies that don't allow anything that can shoot projectiles. On the other hand, if the gun is going to be used for mock combat, then you might consider upgrading the internals to shoot farther. There are tons of sites with tutorials for upgrading NERF guns: SGNerf-Modworks, NerfHaven

WARNING: If you plan to wield a NERF gun or any gun-shaped prop in a public setting, it is recommended that you leave the tip orange to show that it is a toy.
