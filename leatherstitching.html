Leather Stitching

Saddle Stitching


Basics:

Make a series of holes, lined up, in two pieces of leather.
Cut thread approx 5-6 times length to be sewn.
Attach a needle to each end.
Start with center of thread at first hole, one needle on each side.
Pass both needles to other side, pulling tight.
Backstitch at end.

Making holes

Chisel punch
Drill
hole punch
Overstitcher (sprocket wheel)
Awl blade

Thread

Artificial Sinew
Waxed linen thread
cotton thread
tendon

Needles

heavy duty
plenty extra
sharpen as needed

Tips

Rubber cement pieces before punching holes
Pliers to pull needles (grab as close to eye to avoid breaking)
Consistency for speed, good looks.
Gouger will inset stitching, prevent wear and tear on thread.
