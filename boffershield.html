Boffer Shields

This shield is made of wood, but is still light and mobile, and is sufficiently padded for safety in many LARPing games.

Shield Press

I wanted my shield to have a curve, so I built a shield press like the one described here: Shield Press Tutorial

In order for the wood to hold the curve, two pieces of 2'x2'x0.25" plywood are glued together, curved, and once dry will hold the curved shape. Lighter, flatter shields are possible by using only one piece of 1/4" plywood.












Preparing the Blank

Pre-drill all the holes you will be using. I space about a dozen holes along the rim, about 1/2" from the edge, going all the way around. Find a comfortable spot for your handle and strap. Sand down any rough parts of the holes.

If you want to paint your shield with an emblem or heraldry, glue a sheet of white canvas to the front, but don't paint it yet. Its just easier to get that on now before any hardware is attached. For a simple wood grain shield, I use wood stain to get a darker richer color.









Rivet the leather handles to the back of the shield. How to peen rivets - Under Construction








Edging

Zip tie the hose edging to the rim. Use the small zip ties to secure it. Trim the hose at any corner if it would make it stick up.












Edge Padding

Tape insulation foam around the edging.













Arm Padding

Attach foam arm padding on back of shield.
